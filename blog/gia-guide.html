01 · 為什麼買鑽石一定要看 GIA?

GIA(Gemological Institute of America，美國寶石學院)是全球最權威的鑽石鑑定機構，以「公正第三方」身份對每顆鑽石的 4Cs(克拉、淨度、顏色、車工)逐一檢驗。市面上其他鑑定機構(IGI、HRD、AGS)各有特色，但 GIA 仍是國際公認最嚴格、最一致的標準 — 同一顆鑽石送 GIA 比送其他機構，結果常會「等級降一階」。

一個簡單原則：有 GIA 就優先看 GIA;只有 IGI/HRD，要打個小折扣考慮。

02 · 線上驗證鑑定書真偽

拿到實體鑑定書後，永遠先驗證再相信:

  • 找到報告右上角的 Report Number(10 位數字)
  • 連線至 GIA Report Check
  • 輸入 Report Number 與克拉數，系統會即時顯示這份鑑定書的所有數據
  • 把線上資料與紙本一一比對 — 不一致 100% 是偽造

03 · 4Cs:鑽石價格的四大支柱

每份 GIA 鑑定書的核心是 4Cs。記住 4 個 C，你就掌握了鑽石價格 95% 的決定因素。但這 4 個 C 對「閃亮度」的影響其實很不平均:

Cut(車工)★ 決定性
Color(顏色)中等
Clarity(淨度)中等
Carat(克拉)無關
圖 14Cs 對「閃亮度」的相對影響 — 車工幾乎是唯一決定性因素

Carat(克拉)

鑽石重量，1 克拉 = 0.2 公克。價格與克拉呈非線性 — 0.99 克拉與 1.00 克拉差價可達 20%，因為「1 克拉」是心理門檻。買 0.95–0.99 克拉，實際視覺差異極小但價格便宜不少。

圖 2價格在 1.00 ct 出現心理門檻跳價 — 0.99 ct 是 CP 值甜蜜點

Color(顏色)

從 D(無色，最稀有)到 Z(黃)。整條色階如下，顏色等級越往右越偏黃:

D E F G H I J K L M N–Z
無色 D-F ★ G-H 最佳 CP 偏黃 K+
圖 3D 到 Z 顏色階梯 — 肉眼下 D-F 與 G-H 的差距極小，但價差 30-50%

Color(顏色)

從 D(無色，最稀有)到 Z(黃)。

  • D–F:無色等級，最頂級
  • G–J:接近無色，肉眼幾乎看不出
  • K 以下:開始看得到黃色

CP 值最佳區間是 G–H — 視覺上接近 D-F，但價格便宜 30–50%。

Clarity(淨度)

從完美無瑕到肉眼可見內含物:FL → IF → VVS1 → VVS2 → VS1 → VS2 → SI1 → SI2 → I1 → I2 → I3。

VS1–SI1 是 CP 值甜蜜點 — 肉眼看不到瑕疵，但價格比 VVS1 便宜很多。

Cut(車工)

GIA 把車工分為 Excellent / Very Good / Good / Fair / Poor。這是 4Cs 中唯一直接決定「閃亮度」的因素，也是 BrillianceLab 重點分析的部分。

但 — GIA 的 Excellent 範圍其實很廣。同樣標 Excellent 的兩顆鑽石，實際閃亮度可能差 15%。這就是為什麼必須進一步看「比例圖」的數字。

圖 4GIA Excellent 是「範圍很寬」的等級 — 同樣 Excellent 也有真頂級 vs 邊緣之分

04 · 比例圖:BrillianceLab 評分的核心

GIA 鑑定書通常有兩頁，第二頁的比例圖(Proportions)是 BrillianceLab 評分的核心資料來源。比例圖會以剖面圖標示這顆鑽石的所有測量數據。以下四個是必看:

圖 5圓形明亮式鑽石剖面圖 — 紅色標示亭角是「閃亮度第一決定因素」

Table % — 桌面百分比

  • 定義：鑽石頂部最大平面相對於腰圍直徑的比例
  • GIA 標示:Table
  • 理想範圍:55–56%
  • 太大：鑽石看起來「平」，缺少立體感
  • 太小：減少正面反射光量

Depth % — 深度百分比

  • 定義：鑽石總高度相對於腰圍直徑的比例
  • GIA 標示:Depth 或 Total Depth
  • 理想範圍:61–62.5%
  • 太深：中央會看起來黑黑的(光從亭部漏出)
  • 太淺：出現「魚眼」效應，中間像玻璃透明

Crown Angle — 冠部角度

  • 定義：鑽石上半部與腰圍的傾斜角度
  • GIA 標示:Crown Angle
  • 理想範圍:34–35°
  • 主導「火彩 (Fire)」 — 鑽石折射出彩虹光譜的能力
  • 偏離 0.5° 就會明顯影響火彩強度

Pavilion Angle — 亭部角度(最關鍵！)

  • 定義：鑽石下半部與腰圍的傾斜角度
  • GIA 標示:Pavilion Angle
  • 理想範圍:40.6–41°
  • 主導「亮度 (Brilliance)」
  • 偏離 0.1° 就足以讓鑽石失去 5–10% 亮度
圖 6亭角偏離敏感度曲線 — 偏離 0.5° 即可損失 25% 亮度，過深比過淺更糟

這四個數字就是 BrillianceLab 需要的全部 — 把它們輸入主頁的篩選器，就能看到這顆鑽石的客觀光學評分。

05 · 進階:Polish、Symmetry、Fluorescence

Polish(拋光)

鑽石表面打磨的精細程度。理想為 Excellent (EX);Very Good (VG) 仍然極優，Good 以下會明顯影響光澤。

Symmetry(對稱性)

所有刻面是否精準對稱。這是「八心八箭」是否完美的關鍵 — 只有 Symmetry = Excellent 的鑽石，在觀察鏡下才會看到完美的八心八箭圖樣。

Fluorescence(螢光)

鑽石在紫外光下發藍光的強度，從 None / Faint / Medium / Strong / Very Strong:

  • None / Faint:沒影響，放心
  • Medium 以上:對 D–H 顏色等級的鑽石可能讓鑽石看起來「霧霧的」;但對 I–K 顏色等級反而能修飾微黃，效果更好

07 · 常見問題

Q1:沒有 GIA 鑑定書，IGI 或 HRD 可以嗎？

可以，但要記得 IGI 與 HRD 的標準比 GIA 稍寬鬆，等級可能要降一階看待。

Q2:鑽石商說「兩顆都是 Excellent」，但價差很大，為什麼？

因為 Excellent 範圍很廣。一定要看比例圖數字，用 BrillianceLab 計算實際分數，價差通常就是「真 Excellent」與「邊緣 Excellent」的差距。

Q3:亭角偏離理想 0.3°，差很多嗎？

亭角是最敏感的參數。0.3° 偏離大約等於減損 15–25% 亮度。在實體店面用 10× 放大鏡比較，真的會看出差異。

Q4:該選 D-VVS1 還是 G-VS1?

若預算有限，G-VS1 是更聰明的選擇 — 視覺上幾乎一致，但便宜 40–50%。把省下的錢拿去確保「車工」買到頂級，閃亮度提升遠比顏色淨度提升一階更有感。

01 · Why GIA matters

GIA (the Gemological Institute of America) is the world's most authoritative diamond grading lab. As a neutral third party, it grades every diamond on the 4Cs (Carat, Clarity, Color, Cut). Other labs exist (IGI, HRD, AGS) — but GIA remains the gold standard: send the same stone elsewhere and it often comes back "one grade higher."

Rule of thumb: trust GIA first. With IGI/HRD only, mentally discount the grade by one notch.

02 · Verify the report online

Always verify before you trust:

  • Find the Report Number in the top-right corner (10 digits)
  • Visit GIA Report Check
  • Enter the Report Number and carat — every field appears live
  • Match online vs. paper field-by-field. Mismatch = forgery, no exceptions.

03 · The 4Cs explained

The 4Cs control 95% of a diamond's price. Master them and you've mastered the market. But these 4Cs influence brilliance very unevenly:

Cut★ Decisive
ColorMedium
ClarityMedium
CaratNone
Fig 1Relative impact of the 4Cs on brilliance — Cut is essentially the only decisive C

Carat

Weight. 1 carat = 0.2 grams. The price–carat curve is non-linear: 0.99 ct vs 1.00 ct can differ by 20% because "1.00" is a psychological threshold. Buying 0.95–0.99 ct gives near-identical visual size at a real discount.

Color

Graded D (colorless) to Z (yellow).

  • D–F: colorless, top tier
  • G–J: near-colorless, virtually indistinguishable face-up
  • K and below: yellow tint visible

The value sweet spot sits at G–H — looks like D–F, costs 30–50% less.

Clarity

Flawless to eye-visible: FL → IF → VVS1 → VVS2 → VS1 → VS2 → SI1 → SI2 → I1 → I2 → I3.

VS1–SI1 is the value sweet spot — eye-clean, far cheaper than VVS1.

Cut

GIA grades cut as Excellent / Very Good / Good / Fair / Poor. Cut is the only C that directly determines brilliance, and it's what BrillianceLab focuses on.

But — GIA's "Excellent" is a wide bucket. Two stones both labeled Excellent can differ by 15% in real-world brilliance. That's why we have to read the proportions diagram.

04 · The Proportions diagram (BrillianceLab inputs)

A GIA report has two pages. The proportions diagram on page 2 is the data source for BrillianceLab. The diagram shows a cross-section of the stone with all key measurements. Four numbers matter most:

Fig 5Round brilliant cross-section — pavilion (red) is the dominant brilliance driver

Table %

  • What it is: top flat facet expressed as a % of girdle diameter
  • GIA label: Table
  • Ideal: 55–56%
  • Too large: looks "flat", lacks depth
  • Too small: less face-up brightness

Depth %

  • What it is: total height as a % of girdle diameter
  • GIA label: Depth or Total Depth
  • Ideal: 61–62.5%
  • Too deep: dark center (light leaks through pavilion)
  • Too shallow: "fish-eye" transparency

Crown Angle

  • What it is: angle of the upper crown to the girdle
  • GIA label: Crown Angle
  • Ideal: 34–35°
  • Drives Fire — rainbow flashes
  • 0.5° off and Fire visibly weakens

Pavilion Angle (the critical one)

  • What it is: angle of the lower pavilion to the girdle
  • GIA label: Pavilion Angle
  • Ideal: 40.6–41°
  • Drives Brilliance
  • Just 0.1° off can shave 5–10% off brilliance
Fig 6Pavilion sensitivity curve — 0.5° off costs 25% brilliance; deep-side hurts more than shallow

These four numbers are everything BrillianceLab needs. Plug them into the scorer on the home page for an objective optical rating.

05 · Advanced: Polish, Symmetry, Fluorescence

Polish

Surface finish. Ideal is Excellent (EX). Very Good (VG) still excellent. Anything below Good visibly dulls the surface.

Symmetry

How precisely facets align. This is what makes Hearts & Arrows possible — only Symmetry = Excellent stones show clean H&A patterns under a viewer.

Fluorescence

Blue glow under UV. None / Faint / Medium / Strong / Very Strong.

  • None / Faint: no impact, safe
  • Medium and above: on D–H stones, can cause a "milky" look. On I–K stones, often improves appearance by masking yellow tint.

07 · FAQ

Q1. No GIA report — IGI or HRD ok?

Yes, but mentally discount the grade by one notch. IGI and HRD run slightly looser than GIA.

Q2. The seller says both stones are "Excellent" but prices differ widely. Why?

Because "Excellent" is a wide bucket. Read the proportions, run BrillianceLab — that price gap usually mirrors the gap between "true Excellent" and "borderline Excellent."

Q3. Pavilion is 0.3° off ideal — how bad is that?

Pavilion is the most sensitive parameter. 0.3° off ≈ a 15–25% drop in brilliance. Side-by-side under a 10× loupe, the difference is real.

Q4. D-VVS1 vs G-VS1?

On a budget, G-VS1 is the smarter pick — visually nearly identical, 40–50% cheaper. Spend the savings on guaranteed top-tier cut; brilliance gains beat color/clarity gains every time.
