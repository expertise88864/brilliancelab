八心八箭真相
哪些 GIA Excellent 其實不及格

2026.05.01· 閱讀時間 7 分鐘· 作者 BrillianceLab

廣告位 · ADVERTISEMENT

01 · 八心八箭其實不是 GIA 的等級

「八心八箭(Hearts & Arrows, H&A)」是指鑽石在專用觀察鏡下，從上方看可看到八支對稱的箭、從底部看可看到八顆對稱的心。這是日本 1980 年代由 Kazumi Okuda 提出的概念，代表所有 57 個刻面的排列與對稱完美。

但這個圖樣從來不是 GIA 的官方等級。GIA 報告書上不會印出「H&A」，他們只會給你 Cut / Polish / Symmetry 三個等級。是否真正八心八箭，完全要靠比例 + Symmetry 等級 + 觀察鏡才能驗證。

結論:GIA 不會幫你驗證八心八箭，你必須自己用比例數字算，或要求賣家提供八心八箭觀察鏡照片(IdealScope 或 H&A Viewer)。

02 · 為什麼 GIA Excellent 範圍這麼寬？

GIA 的 Cut Grade 是用面光區段表(Cut Grade Tables)判斷的，只要每項參數都落在他們設定的範圍內，就算 Excellent。問題是這個範圍非常寬鬆:

圖 1GIA Excellent 接受的亭角跨度高達 1.2°，但真正能形成八心八箭的範圍只有約 0.4°

類似的事情發生在四項參數的每一項。這就是「真 Excellent」與「邊緣 Excellent」之間的差距，也是 BrillianceLab 想揭露的真相。

04 · 真八心八箭的數學條件

從 1919 年 Marcel Tolkowsky 的論文開始，業界已經知道光學完美的圓形明亮式鑽石需要同時滿足以下條件:

  • 桌面 (Table %):54–57%（理想 55–56%）
  • 深度 (Depth %):60.5–62.5%（理想 61–62.3%）
  • 冠角 (Crown):33.7–35.5°（理想 34–35°）
  • 亭角 (Pavilion):40.5–41.0°（理想 40.6–40.9°）
  • 冠 + 2 × 亭 ≈ 115.5–117° 之間的調和值
  • Symmetry = Excellent;Polish = Excellent / Very Good
  • Star Length ≈ 50%,Lower Girdle ≈ 75–80%

大部分網購鑽石無法當下用觀察鏡看八心八箭，但只要拿到鑑定書的 PDF，你就可以:

  1. 抄下 Table、Depth、Crown Angle、Pavilion Angle 四項數字

06 · 常見問題

Q1:我看到的鑽石商說有「八心八箭證書」，可信嗎？

「八心八箭」沒有國際標準。不同店家的證書標準不一，有些比 GIA Excellent 嚴格，有些比 GIA 還寬鬆。務必索取數字 + 觀察鏡照片，並用 BrillianceLab 自己驗算。

Q2:Triple Excellent + GIA 不就夠了嗎？

Triple X 只代表 Cut / Polish / Symmetry 都是 Excellent，但並沒有規定比例必須在甜蜜點內。Cut: Excellent 的範圍寬達 1.2° 亭角差距，光學表現可以差 25%。

Q3:邊緣 Excellent 看起來真的差很多嗎？

並排比較時，差別清楚到肉眼可見。獨自欣賞時可能不明顯 — 但你買的是一輩子戴的鑽石，前 30 秒不會差，但時間久了你會察覺它「沒那麼閃」。為了多一點預算的差距買到真八心八箭，絕對值得。

01 · "Hearts & Arrows" is not actually a GIA grade

Hearts & Arrows (H&A) refers to the optical pattern visible under a special viewer: eight symmetric arrows from the top, eight symmetric hearts from below. The concept was introduced in the 1980s by Kazumi Okuda and represents perfect alignment of all 57 facets.

But this pattern was never an official GIA grade. GIA reports only print Cut / Polish / Symmetry. Whether a stone is truly H&A is determined by its proportions, Symmetry grade, and an actual viewer photo.

Bottom line: GIA won't verify Hearts & Arrows for you. You have to compute it yourself from the proportion numbers, or insist on an IdealScope / H&A viewer photo from the seller.

02 · Why is GIA "Excellent" such a wide bucket?

GIA assigns Cut Grade via tables: as long as each parameter falls within their accepted range, you get Excellent. The catch — those ranges are very loose:

Fig 1GIA Excellent spans 1.2° on pavilion alone — true H&A territory is a much narrower 0.4° band.

The same sloppiness applies to all four parameters. This is the gap between "true Excellent" and "borderline Excellent" — and what BrillianceLab is built to expose.

04 · The math of true Hearts & Arrows

Since Marcel Tolkowsky's 1919 paper, an optically perfect round brilliant must satisfy:

  • Table %: 54–57% (ideal 55–56%)
  • Depth %: 60.5–62.5% (ideal 61–62.3%)
  • Crown: 33.7–35.5° (ideal 34–35°)
  • Pavilion: 40.5–41.0° (ideal 40.6–40.9°)
  • Crown + 2 × Pavilion ≈ 115.5–117°
  • Symmetry = Excellent; Polish = Excellent / Very Good
  • Star Length ≈ 50%, Lower Girdle ≈ 75–80%

Most online stones can't be inspected with a viewer on the spot. With the report PDF, you can still:

  2. Confirm Symmetry = Excellent
  3. Plug into BrillianceLab

  5. Verify Crown + 2 × Pavilion lands in 115.5–117

06 · FAQ

Q1. The seller has a "Hearts & Arrows certificate." Trustworthy?

There is no international standard. Different stores use different bars; some stricter than GIA, others looser. Get the numbers + a viewer photo and re-run BrillianceLab yourself.

Q2. Isn't Triple Excellent on a GIA report enough?

Triple X means Cut / Polish / Symmetry all Excellent — but doesn't require sweet-spot proportions. Cut: Excellent spans 1.2° on pavilion alone, with up to 25% brilliance gap.

Q3. Does borderline Excellent really look that different?

Side-by-side, the gap is obvious. In isolation, less so — but this is a stone you'll wear for life. The difference creeps up on you. Spending a little more for a true H&A is almost always worth it.
